The Evidence

Seven Domains. One Equation.

Cross-domain validation of the Cx operator

The Cx operator — Cx = Φ × C² — was tested against real-world data from seven independent physical systems. The same mathematical structure produces statistically significant results across all seven. No parameter tuning between domains. No domain-specific adjustments. One equation.

Cx = Φ × C²

The domains were chosen to maximize diversity: if the operator works across systems this different, the underlying structure is likely fundamental rather than domain-specific.

The Seven Domains

1. Solar Wind

Heliophysics · NASA data

Measured coherence in solar wind plasma flow. The Cx operator identifies coherent structures in turbulent plasma — regions where magnetic field lines maintain organized behavior despite surrounding chaos. These structures correspond to known physical phenomena (coronal mass ejections, sector boundaries).

2. Neural Activity

Neuroscience · EEG / fMRI literature

Applied the Cx operator to neural integration and coherence data. The operator’s predictions align with established neuroscience findings: higher Cx values correspond to states associated with conscious awareness, lower values to unconscious states. Structurally parallel to the Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI) validated by Casali et al. (2013).

3. Jet Streams

Atmospheric Science · NOAA reanalysis data

Jet stream behavior exhibits measurable coherence patterns. The Cx operator identifies transitions between laminar (coherent) and turbulent (fragmented) flow regimes. These transitions correspond to known meteorological events and have predictive implications for weather pattern stability.

4. Seismic Activity

Geophysics · USGS seismic data

Seismic wave propagation through geological structures shows coherence signatures detectable by the Cx operator. Coherence values correlate with structural integrity of geological formations and the coupling between tectonic elements.

5. Financial Markets

Econophysics · Market microstructure data

Market data exhibits coherence signatures that distinguish between stable trading regimes and pre-crash fragmentation. The Cx operator detects the same integration-coherence relationship in financial time series that it detects in plasma. The math doesn’t know what domain it’s in.

6. Ocean Currents

Physical Oceanography · NOAA/satellite data

Ocean circulation patterns — from thermohaline currents to eddy formation — display measurable coherence that the Cx operator quantifies. Coherent structures in ocean flow correspond to known oceanographic phenomena and maintain stability over time.

7. Power Grids

Electrical Engineering · Grid stability data

Electrical power grids are distributed systems where coherence (phase synchronization across generators) directly determines stability. The Cx operator measures integration and coherence in grid behavior. Grid failures correspond to coherence collapse — the same signature the operator detects in every other domain.
